Pain Points Identified
  • Users felt that the application was not intuitive and had a confusing home page.
  • The application crashed repeatedly during use.
  • The UI felt immature and didn't compel users to engage.
  • The notifications were not working properly.
  • Users didn't understand certain elements of the app, such as what the data graphs represented.

*Data collected through a feedback form

03. My Strategy for Addressing the Challenges

After the pilot, users found the app's UI and certain features unintuitive. I led the next design steps by brainstorming with the Product Owner and Tech team to address these challenges.

Our conversation went like this
Lupita Almuhana, Product Owner & Founder

Looks like users feel the UI is very immature and not very intuitive — any idea what we can do?

Identity Protected, Tech Lead / AI Engineer

Why don't we just fix the bugs, change some colors — I'm not sure what's so confusing about the graphs?

Me, Founding Product Designer

There's a lot to improve. The reason users found it unintuitive is that the app was text-heavy, lacked clear visual hierarchy, and had poor information architecture.

Lupita Almuhana, Product Owner & Founder

Ok, then what can we do?

Me, Founding Product Designer

Yes, I got a plan for it, hear me out....

My Approach

I analyzed the information architecture, identified friction points, and proposed alternatives. Users found the UI immature due to inconsistencies in color, text hierarchy, and signifiers, leading us to a full redesign. This time, I focused on these core foundational principles:

User Centricity

With a clear understanding of user preferences, I improved existing features, fixed pain points, and ensured a user-centric redesign through focus group testing.

Seamless and Stunning

I aimed for more than usability—I wanted seamless interactions where every transition felt meaningful, creating a wow-worthy experience. To ensure this, I tested it with a small focus group.

04. Experiences I Designed With My Approach
Onboarding Experience 01

We start by personalizing the experience—understanding what uplifts users to better identify their stressors. Each step adapts in real time, tailoring the next screen based on their input to keep them engaged and supported.

Onboarding Experience 02

Here, I designed a stress landscape assessment with 27 in-depth questions to build a deeper understanding of each user's stressors. To keep the experience engaging, I structured the flow with a short break after every 10 questions, featuring a simple exercise to help users reset before continuing.

Core Experience

Once onboarding is complete, users step into their personalized daily wellness hub. The core experience delivers intelligent, real-time stress interventions—from AI-guided coping strategies and quick micro-resets to daily mood check-ins—all tailored to each user's unique stress landscape and continuously updated as they grow.

05. What Users Loved About the New Experience

With Stressie's pilots at Amazon and InKind, we gathered early feedback and data on the revised experience. Here's what they loved most.

" I love the app's interface. Even before exploring its features, the overall vibe instantly makes me feel calmer and less stressed. "

" The interventions are simple yet effective— I don't need anything extra, just my chair and a little space. "

" The Quick Resets are a game-changer! They're perfect for moments of high stress, and I especially love the box breathing exercise. "

" What surprised me most was how the app actually learns my patterns — it checks in at exactly the right moment and the advice never feels generic. "

" I went from feeling constantly overwhelmed to managing my day in short, intentional moments. The daily check-ins are now part of my routine. "

06. Frustrations Users Experienced

As part of the same pilot, we also asked users about any frustrations they had with the app. Here's what they shared.

" There's no option to pause the stress assessment, and I'm unsure if my progress saves when I exit. This is frustrating. "

" I didn't find the coping points compelling as an incentive to complete the onboarding. I don't know their value. "

" Answering the questions feels mentally exhausting. I haven't built trust with the app yet, so sharing personal feelings this early is uncomfortable. "
